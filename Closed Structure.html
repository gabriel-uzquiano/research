Structured propositions

If the failures of Structure are confined to propositions that are not expressible by closed expressions of the language, then if we momentarily set aside demonstratives and anaphora, there might still be hope for the thought that complete sentences of English express structured propositions.

Propositional attitude reports

If two sentences differ in syntactic structure or some of their corresponding syntactic constituents differ in denotation, then they are not intersubstitutable in propositional attitude reports. So, if two sentences are intersubstitutable, then they share the same syntactic structure and corresponding syntactic constituents share the same denotation. Two responses

We anticipate at least two lines of response to the inconsistency.

Deny the coherence of plural propositional quantification

We may be in a position to understand propositional quantification as quantification into sentence position, but we cross the line when we attempt to make sense of plural propositional quantification.

The motivation for this position will largely depend on one's attitude to higher-order logic. We understand higher-order quantification in its own terms, and our competence with higher-order languages is not mediated by a translation into natural language. The interpretation of higher-order languages is constrained by a variety of metasemantic factors, but at the end of the day, the ability to make sense of higher-order languages should be at least partly judged on the basis of the success of theorizing in such languages. A similar speech appears to be available for plural propositional quantification, whose interpretation is subject to similar metasemantic constraints.
